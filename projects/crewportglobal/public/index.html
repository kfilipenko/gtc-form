For employers

Employer-side clients describe demand and buying context.

  • Company and vessel context are collected after authenticated access.
  • Crew need is recorded through rank, vessel, salary, joining date and contract conditions.
  • Authority evidence defines who may act for the company or request.
  • Only reviewed demand can be published or used for candidate presentation.
  • The employer-side client remains the main payer for core services.
  • Operational access is scoped to the client, company, vessel or assigned task.

For seafarers

Seafarers provide professional supply data.

  • Rank, department, availability and contact readiness support matching.
  • Documents and certificates are reviewed as part of professional readiness.
  • Expected compensation and contract preferences are compared with demand.
  • Candidate presentation is not automatic and requires human review.
  • Seafarer data is not visible to broad groups by default.
  • Mandatory recruitment or placement fees for seafarers are prohibited.

How the platform works

Registration, authentication and authorization are separate steps.

  • Registration creates a physical-person and user card.
  • Authentication confirms access to the service account and validates contact channels.
  • Authorization grants roles, visibility and rights only on proven grounds.
  • The personal cabinet is assembled from confirmed capability cards.
