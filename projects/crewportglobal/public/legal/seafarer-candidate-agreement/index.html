This Seafarer Candidate Agreement defines the terms under which a seafarer, maritime professional or prospective candidate may create and maintain a profile on CrewPortGlobal, upload professional documents, participate in document readiness workflows and be considered for matching with crew requests submitted by shipowners, vessel operators, ship managers or authorized maritime employers.

CrewPortGlobal is developed and operated by GTC INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY FZ-LLC as a digital maritime crew data, documentation and matching platform.

At Stage 1, CrewPortGlobal provides digital profile management, document collection, document readiness support, AI-assisted data structuring and candidate matching workflows. CrewPortGlobal does not represent itself as a licensed manning agency, employment agency or seafarer recruitment agency unless and until required approvals are confirmed by competent authorities or relevant services are provided through licensed partners.

Core no-fee statement

CrewPortGlobal does not charge seafarers recruitment, placement or employment fees.

You do not have to pay CrewPortGlobal to be considered for crew opportunities through the platform.

You do not have to purchase optional services from CrewPortGlobal in order to create a profile, be visible in the matching pool or be considered for a relevant crew request.

Any optional paid services offered to seafarers must be clearly separated from recruitment or placement and must not be a condition for access to employment opportunities.

If anyone asks you to pay a recruitment, placement or employment fee in connection with CrewPortGlobal, you should report it immediately through the Complaint Handling Procedure.

Definitions

For the purpose of this Agreement:

Platform means CrewPortGlobal.com and related systems, databases, forms, chat interfaces, workflows, APIs, documents and services operated by GTC INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY FZ-LLC.

Candidate means a seafarer, maritime professional or prospective maritime worker who creates a profile or submits documents through the Platform.

Seafarer Profile means the digital professional profile created for a Candidate, including personal details, professional experience, certificates, availability, documents and other submitted information.

Crew Request means a request submitted by a shipowner, vessel operator, ship manager, crew manager or maritime employer for one or more crew positions.

Matching means the process of comparing Candidate data with Crew Request requirements.

Shortlist means a list of Candidates identified as potentially relevant to a Crew Request.

GTC_USER_ID means the internal user identifier assigned by the GTC system to a registered person or entity.

Document Readiness means the platform status indicating whether documents have been uploaded, reviewed, are complete, expired, missing or require clarification.

Optional Seafarer Services means paid services offered to Candidates that are not recruitment, placement or employment services and are not required for job access.

Recruitment Fee means any fee, charge or deduction imposed on a seafarer as a condition for access to employment, placement or recruitment.

Platform role

CrewPortGlobal helps Candidates organize professional maritime information and participate in structured crew matching workflows.

The Platform may:

  1. Create a Candidate profile.
  2. Assign or connect a GTC_USER_ID.
  3. Collect professional and identity information.
  4. Collect maritime certificates and documents.
  5. Help structure work experience by rank, vessel type and contract history.
  6. Support document readiness review.
  7. Compare Candidate data with Crew Requests.
  8. Prepare Candidate summaries.
  9. Allow human-reviewed submission to shipowners or maritime employers.
  10. Store workflow history and compliance records.
  11. Provide communication and support functions.

Unless expressly stated otherwise, CrewPortGlobal does not employ the Candidate and does not guarantee employment, contracts, salary, joining date, visa, flag-state acceptance, medical approval, travel approval or final selection by any shipowner or employer.

Candidate eligibility

By using the Platform, the Candidate confirms that:

  1. The Candidate is legally able to provide the information submitted.
  2. The Candidate has authority to upload the documents submitted.
  3. The Candidate will provide accurate, current and truthful information.
  4. The Candidate will not submit forged, altered, stolen or misleading documents.
  5. The Candidate will update the Platform if important information changes.
  6. The Candidate will not use the Platform for fraud, misrepresentation or unlawful purposes.

CrewPortGlobal may suspend or reject a profile if there is evidence of false information, fraudulent documents, unlawful use, identity misuse or serious compliance risk.

  2. Seaman book.

  4. STCW certificates.
  5. Certificate of Competency.
  6. Endorsements.

  12. Vaccination records where required.
  13. Previous contracts or sea service records.
  14. References.
  15. Other maritime professional documents.

The Candidate is responsible for ensuring that uploaded documents are valid, accurate and legally obtained.

Document readiness status

CrewPortGlobal may apply status labels to uploaded documents, including:

  1. Uploaded.
  2. Pending review.
  3. Verified.
  4. Expired.
  5. Rejected.
  6. Needs clarification.
  7. Externally verified.
  8. Partner verified.

A document readiness status helps organize the Candidate profile and support matching workflows.

Unless expressly stated, a status does not guarantee that an issuing authority, flag state, port authority, insurer, class society or employer will accept the document.

The Candidate agrees to:

  1. Provide truthful information.
  2. Keep profile data updated.
  3. Upload only genuine documents.
  4. Not impersonate another person.
  5. Not submit documents belonging to another person.
  6. Respond to reasonable requests for clarification.
  7. Respect other Platform users.
  8. Not attempt unauthorized access to Platform systems.
  9. Not use the Platform for fraudulent or unlawful purposes.
  10. Report suspected fake jobs, prohibited fees or misuse of documents.

No guarantee of employment

Creating a profile on CrewPortGlobal does not guarantee:

  1. Employment.
  2. Contract offer.
  3. Shortlisting.
  4. Interview.
  5. Salary level.
  6. Visa approval.
  7. Joining approval.
  8. Flag-state approval.
  9. Acceptance by any shipowner, operator, manager or employer.

CrewPortGlobal supports structured profile management and matching workflows, but final employment decisions are made by shipowners, employers, vessel operators, ship managers or authorized hiring parties.

Matching and shortlisting

CrewPortGlobal may use structured data, human review and AI-assisted tools to compare Candidate profiles with Crew Requests.

Matching may consider:

  1. Rank.
  2. Department.
  3. Certificates.
  4. Endorsements.
  5. Vessel type experience.
  6. Contract history.
  7. Availability.
  8. Location.
  9. Language skills.
  10. Salary expectations.
  11. Medical or document validity indicators.
  12. Client-specific requirements.
  13. Compliance and risk flags.

The Candidate understands that matching is a support process and does not create a right to employment or contract.

Sharing Candidate profile with shipowners

CrewPortGlobal may share relevant Candidate information with shipowners, vessel operators, ship managers, crew managers or authorized maritime employers for crew matching and assessment purposes.

Where possible, the Candidate should be informed when a profile is being considered or submitted for a specific opportunity.

The shared information may include:

  1. Name.
  2. Rank.
  3. Nationality.
  4. Professional experience.
  5. Availability.
  6. Certificates and document readiness status.
  7. Contact information where appropriate.
  8. Candidate summary.
  9. Uploaded documents where necessary and permitted.

CrewPortGlobal should not share Candidate data for unrelated marketing or non-maritime purposes without appropriate consent or lawful basis.

Optional paid services

CrewPortGlobal may offer optional services to Candidates under a transparent price list.

Examples may include:

  1. CV preparation.
  2. Maritime profile improvement support.
  3. Document translation.
  4. Certificate expiry reminders.
  5. Secure document vault features.
  6. Interview preparation.
  7. Training information or referrals.

These services are optional.

They do not guarantee employment or selection.

The Candidate must not purchase any optional service if anyone presents it as a mandatory condition for employment through CrewPortGlobal.

Prohibited fees and reporting

CrewPortGlobal prohibits recruitment, placement or employment fees charged to Candidates.

The Candidate should report any request for payment that appears to be connected with access to a job, contract, shortlist or employment opportunity.

Examples of prohibited conduct may include:

  1. “Pay to be shortlisted.”
  2. “Pay to get a job.”
  3. “Pay to receive a contract.”
  4. “Pay to be introduced to a shipowner.”
  5. “Pay from your first salary.”
  6. “Pay an agent fee to join the vessel.”
  7. “Buy this service or you will not be considered.”

Complaints may be submitted through the CrewPortGlobal Complaint Handling Procedure.

Communication with shipowners and partners

The Candidate may communicate with shipowners, operators, managers, partners or CrewPortGlobal team members through approved Platform channels or external channels where authorized.

The Candidate should be cautious of:

  1. Unverified job offers.
  2. Requests to send money.
  3. Requests to surrender original documents.
  4. Unofficial email domains.
  5. Pressure to act immediately.
  6. Requests to bypass the Platform for suspicious reasons.
  7. Contract terms that differ from those presented earlier.

Suspicious communications should be reported.

Candidate account suspension

CrewPortGlobal may suspend, restrict or deactivate a Candidate profile if:

  1. False information is submitted.
  2. Fraudulent documents are uploaded.
  3. The Candidate impersonates another person.
  4. The Candidate misuses Platform systems.
  5. The Candidate harasses or threatens other users.
  6. The Candidate attempts unauthorized access.
  7. The Candidate participates in fraud or unlawful activity.
  8. Legal or compliance risk requires suspension.
  9. The Candidate requests deactivation.

Where appropriate, CrewPortGlobal may allow the Candidate to provide clarification before final action is taken.

Candidate profile deactivation

The Candidate may request profile deactivation.

Upon deactivation:

  1. The profile may no longer be visible for new matching workflows.
  2. Certain data may be retained for legal, security, audit, complaint or compliance reasons.
  3. Uploaded documents may be deleted or restricted according to the Data Retention and Deletion Policy.
  4. Active complaints, disputes or legal holds may delay deletion.

AI-assisted processing

CrewPortGlobal may use AI tools to:

  1. Ask onboarding questions.
  2. Structure Candidate data.
  3. Summarize experience.
  4. Identify missing documents.
  5. Compare Candidate information with Crew Request requirements.
  6. Prepare draft profile summaries.
  7. Support support-team review.

AI output may contain errors.

The Candidate may request correction of inaccurate profile summaries or data.

Serious decisions, including complaint decisions, fraud flags, document rejection and Candidate submission for final review, should involve human review.

Human review

CrewPortGlobal may apply human review before:

  1. Presenting a Candidate to a shipowner.
  2. Marking documents as verified.
  3. Rejecting important documents.
  4. Applying serious compliance flags.
  5. Suspending an account.
  6. Resolving serious complaints.
  7. Handling prohibited fee reports.

Human review is a core part of the CrewPortGlobal compliance-first model.

Candidate responsibility for final decisions

The Candidate is responsible for reviewing any proposed employment terms before accepting a contract.

The Candidate should check:

  1. Employer identity.
  2. Vessel identity.
  3. Contract duration.
  4. Salary.
  5. Rank and duties.
  6. Joining port.
  7. Travel arrangements.
  8. Insurance and welfare arrangements.
  9. Repatriation terms.
  10. Applicable collective agreement, if any.
  11. Any deductions or charges.
  12. Whether the contract matches the information provided during the matching process.

CrewPortGlobal may support document and workflow organization, but the Candidate must make an informed decision before accepting employment.
