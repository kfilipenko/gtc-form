# CrewPortGlobal — Identity, Business and Vessel Verification Policy

This policy describes the verification baseline used by CrewPortGlobal across the main trust surfaces of the platform: seafarer identity and professional readiness, business-client onboarding, and vessel-related workflow validation.

## 1. Purpose

CrewPortGlobal uses verification controls to reduce fraud, impersonation, sanctions exposure, unauthorized access to candidate data, misleading vessel claims and workflow misuse.

The goal is not to create a separate trust layer for each participant group, but one coherent verification model for both sides of the crew workflow.

## 2. Scope

This policy may apply to:

- seafarers and candidate profiles;
- shipowners, vessel operators, ship managers, crew managers and other business users;
- authorized representatives acting for a business client;
- vessels, vessel data and operational context where candidate presentation depends on them.

## 3. Seafarer identity and professional readiness

CrewPortGlobal may request or review one or more of the following:

1. legal name and contact details;
2. passport or other identity evidence where justified;
3. certificates of competency, endorsements and training records;
4. medical, contract-history or availability context where relevant;
5. document validity, expiry and consistency checks;
6. practical readiness signals for the requested rank, department or vessel type.

Verification does not guarantee employment or embarkation. It supports a more reliable matching workflow and helps reduce false or incomplete candidate submissions.

## 4. Business client KYB

CrewPortGlobal may verify:

1. company legal name and registration details;
2. jurisdiction of incorporation and operating address;
3. representative identity and authority;
4. beneficial ownership or control context where needed;
5. sanctions, embargo or prohibited-party exposure;
6. vessel ownership, management or crewing authority where relevant.

Where authority, legitimacy or risk cannot be reasonably clarified, CrewPortGlobal may pause onboarding, request additional evidence, narrow access or refuse service.

## 5. Vessel verification

Where a workflow is linked to a named vessel or fleet, CrewPortGlobal may review:

1. vessel name, IMO number or equivalent identifier;
2. flag, owner, operator or manager details;
3. whether the named business user appears connected to the vessel or recruitment instruction;
4. whether the vessel context is coherent with the requested role, trade or operational timeline.

Vessel verification is risk-based. It may be lightweight for low-risk workflows and more detailed where ownership, management, sanctions or legitimacy questions arise.

## 6. Risk-based review depth

Verification depth may increase when:

- identity evidence is incomplete or inconsistent;
- a representative cannot demonstrate authority;
- sanctions or reputational concerns arise;
- vessel context appears contradictory or unsupported;
- a request is unusually sensitive or high-value;
- documents appear misleading, altered or materially outdated.

## 7. Outcomes of review

Following review, CrewPortGlobal may:

1. approve onboarding or workflow access;
2. request additional supporting evidence;
3. limit access to specific workflow steps;
4. suspend a case pending clarification;
5. refuse or terminate service where risk is unacceptable.

## 8. Records, privacy and retention

Verification records may be retained for audit, compliance, dispute handling, fraud prevention and platform integrity purposes, subject to the Privacy Policy and applicable legal requirements.

Verification status labels may be used operationally, including labels such as received, under review, incomplete, expired, not confirmed or verified.

## 9. Policy updates

This policy may be revised as CrewPortGlobal expands its identity, business-client and vessel verification controls, or introduces more detailed onboarding and compliance workflows.
