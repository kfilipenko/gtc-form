Shipowner-Agent Framework Agreement

Standard in-platform agreement for appointing an agent to represent a shipowner inside CrewPortGlobal.

Primary focus Agent appointment
Documents home Open shipowner workspace

This page publishes the CrewPortGlobal standard framework used when a shipowner invites a registered agent to manage shipowner-side work on the platform. The document is intended to operate as a platform accession agreement: the legal relationship is created by in-system offer and acceptance, while commercial terms are agreed separately.

1. Parties and accession format

The agreement is concluded between the shipowner or other authorized shipowner-side participant and the agent or agent company selected in CrewPortGlobal. The shipowner initiates the offer inside the platform. The agent accepts the published framework terms inside the platform. CrewPortGlobal records the agreement event, authority evidence, assignment status and notifications.

2. What this agreement does

The agreement creates a controlled representation basis inside CrewPortGlobal. It allows the agent to perform operational actions for the shipowner only within the authority and assignment scope recorded by the platform. External contracts, emails or powers of attorney may be stored as evidence, but they do not automatically activate platform management rights unless the platform assignment is created.

3. Platform activation rule

  • the shipowner must initiate the offer inside CrewPortGlobal;
  • the agent must accept the framework agreement inside CrewPortGlobal;
  • the platform records verified authority evidence;
  • only one active managing agent may control the same shipowner object at a time;
  • the represented participant must be able to see important notifications and intervene through controlled replacement or revocation procedures.

4. Commercial terms are separate

This framework agreement does not impose a fixed service price. Commercial terms, service fees, success fees, SLA, billing basis and invoices must be agreed separately through a Service Order, commercial addendum, request or approved price-basis record.

5. Scope of agent work

The agent may assist the shipowner with crew search, vessel or vacancy preparation, document coordination, contract preparation support and communication with seafarers, but only within the authority, assignment and data-responsibility scope recorded in the platform. The agent must act in good faith, keep the interests of represented parties visible, and must not use the appointment to hide conflicts of interest or bypass participant consent.

6. Notifications and governance rights

CrewPortGlobal records material events in the participant governance notification ledger. The shipowner should receive notice when an offer is sent, accepted, rejected, revoked, replaced, or when an authority or assignment status changes. The shipowner keeps governance rights to review important events and to revoke or replace the agent through controlled platform procedures.

7. Protection of seafarers and shipowners

The agreement must not be used to bypass mandatory seafarer protections, MLC-aligned no-fee rules, wage obligations, insurance, repatriation, family protection or the seafarer employment agreement. Contract-critical employment terms remain subject to party review and applicable law.

8. Acceptance text

I agree to the terms of the framework accession agreement.

9. Current implementation status

CrewPortGlobal currently supports the first runtime slice: shipowner offer, agent acceptance, authority record, active assignment and notification ledger records. Future slices will add commercial Service Orders, agent replacement/revocation, seafarer-side representation and full generated contract documents.
