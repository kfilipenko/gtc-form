Shipowner-agent framework agreement preview

This is the standard CrewPortGlobal framework package used before a shipowner can transfer operational management of a shipowner-side object to an agent inside the platform.

Core Terms

Standard platform form

The agreement is concluded inside CrewPortGlobal as a standard framework form. External contracts may be stored as evidence, but they do not automatically activate platform management rights.

Agent authority

The agent receives operational access only after the offer is accepted, authority evidence is recorded and the platform creates an active assignment.

Commercial terms

Service price, billing basis, success fee, SLA and invoices are agreed separately through a Service Order, commercial addendum or approved price basis.

One active manager

Only one managing representative may control the delegated shipowner object at the same time. Replacement and revocation must be recorded by the platform.

Participant Protection

  • The shipowner sends an offer; the agent accepts the framework terms before management starts.
  • The participant keeps governance rights to view, replace or revoke representation through controlled platform procedures.
  • CrewPortGlobal records notification ledger events for offer, acceptance, authority and assignment milestones.
  • Seafarer employment terms, MLC protections, repatriation, insurance and wage conditions remain controlled by the seafarer employment agreement and applicable law.
