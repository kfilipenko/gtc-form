## Scope

This document defines what CrewPortGlobal is, what it is not, and how the platform is positioned during Stage 1.

## Definitions

- CrewPortGlobal: a digital maritime crew data, document and matching platform.
- Seafarer: an individual creating a professional maritime profile on the platform.
- Shipowner: a shipowner, operator, ship manager, crew manager or maritime employer using the platform for crew-related workflows.
- Stage 1: the initial launch phase focused on digital infrastructure, documentation and matching support.

## Positioning rules

- CrewPortGlobal is operated by GTC INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY FZ-LLC.
- The platform is launched as a technology and workflow service.
- The platform supports profile creation, document collection, candidate review and matching workflows.
- The platform does not present itself as a licensed manning agency or employment agency unless and until required approvals are obtained.
- Recruitment or placement activity requiring licensing will only be performed after regulatory confirmation or through appropriately licensed partners.
- The core revenue model is B2B.
- Seafarers do not pay recruitment or placement fees to access opportunities on the platform.

## Operational workflow

1. Seafarers create and maintain their professional profiles.
2. Shipowners submit crew requests or sourcing instructions.
3. The platform assists with structured intake, document review and candidate matching.
4. Human review is required before any final candidate submission.
5. Compliance-sensitive activities are escalated for manual review.

## Responsibility

- Product owner defines the public positioning of the platform.
- Legal review confirms whether any wording should be narrowed or expanded.
- Operations team ensures that actual workflows remain aligned with the published positioning.
