## 1. Purpose

This Complaint Handling Procedure defines how CrewPortGlobal receives, records, reviews, resolves and documents complaints from seafarers, shipowners, vessel operators, ship managers, partners and other platform users.

The procedure is designed to support a fair, transparent and accountable digital maritime crew matching platform.

CrewPortGlobal is developed and operated by GTC INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY FZ-LLC as a digital maritime crew data, documentation and matching platform.

At Stage 1, CrewPortGlobal does not represent itself as a licensed manning agency or seafarer recruitment agency unless and until required approvals are confirmed by the competent authorities or services are provided through licensed partners.

## 2. Core principles

CrewPortGlobal applies the following principles when handling complaints:

1. Accessibility — users must have a clear and simple way to submit a complaint.
2. Fairness — each complaint must be reviewed without retaliation or discrimination.
3. Timeliness — complaints must be acknowledged and processed within defined timeframes.
4. Confidentiality — complaint data must be protected and shared only with persons who need it for review or resolution.
5. Evidence-based review — decisions should be based on available documents, records, communications and verified facts.
6. No recruitment fees — complaints about prohibited recruitment, placement or employment fees must receive priority review.
7. Human review — serious complaints must not be resolved by automated AI output alone.
8. Record keeping — all complaints and outcomes must be logged for audit and compliance purposes.

## 3. Who may submit a complaint

A complaint may be submitted by:

- a registered seafarer;
- a prospective seafarer;
- a shipowner;
- a vessel operator;
- a ship manager;
- a crew manager;
- an authorized company representative;
- a partner or service provider;
- a person affected by misuse of the platform;
- a regulator, authority or legal representative.

CrewPortGlobal may also open an internal complaint or incident case based on monitoring, audit findings or suspected misconduct.

## 4. Complaint categories

Complaints may include, but are not limited to:

### 4.1 No recruitment fees / payment misconduct

- suspected request for a recruitment fee from a seafarer;
- suspected request for a placement fee from a seafarer;
- wage deduction connected with recruitment or placement;
- hidden payment request by a partner, agent or intermediary;
- paid service presented as mandatory for employment.

### 4.2 False or misleading job information

- fake vacancy;
- misleading contract terms;
- incorrect salary information;
- undisclosed vessel, flag or employer details;
- fraudulent job offer;
- impersonation of CrewPortGlobal or a shipowner.

### 4.3 Document and profile issues

- incorrect document status;
- rejected document dispute;
- suspected fake certificate;
- unauthorized use of uploaded documents;
- incorrect professional profile information;
- request to correct or delete profile data.

### 4.4 Shipowner / employer conduct

- non-payment or delayed payment allegations;
- unsafe work conditions reported by a seafarer;
- contract terms different from those presented during the matching process;
- refusal to provide necessary joining information;
- failure to support repatriation where applicable.

### 4.5 Partner misconduct

- training provider misconduct;
- medical center misconduct;
- travel or visa service misconduct;
- licensed crewing partner misconduct;
- excessive or undisclosed partner fees;
- partner failure to cooperate in complaint review.

### 4.6 Privacy and data protection

- misuse of personal data;
- unauthorized disclosure of documents;
- incorrect data processing;
- failure to respond to data correction or deletion request;
- suspected security incident.

### 4.7 Platform and AI-related issues

- AI-generated inaccurate summary;
- incorrect matching recommendation;
- technical failure affecting a profile, request or payment;
- access issue;
- payment or billing error.

## 6. Minimum information required

The complaint form should request the following information:

1. Full name.
2. Email address.
3. Phone or messaging contact.
4. User role: seafarer, shipowner, partner, other.
5. GTC_USER_ID, if available.
6. Company name, if applicable.
7. Vessel name and IMO number, if applicable.
8. Short description of the complaint.
9. Date of the event.
10. Persons or companies involved.
11. Documents, screenshots or evidence.
12. Desired outcome.
13. Consent to process complaint-related data.

A complaint should not be rejected solely because some information is missing. If the complaint contains enough information to identify the issue, CrewPortGlobal should open a case and request additional details.

## 7. Complaint registration

Each complaint must be registered in the internal system with a unique case number.

Recommended case ID format:

```text
CPG-COMP-YYYY-NNNN
```

Example:

```text
CPG-COMP-2026-0001
```

The complaint record should include:

- case ID;
- date received;
- complainant name;
- complainant role;
- GTC_USER_ID, if available;
- related shipowner / vessel / partner;
- complaint category;
- priority level;
- assigned reviewer;
- status;
- documents and evidence;
- actions taken;
- outcome;
- closure date.

## 8. Priority levels

CrewPortGlobal should classify complaints by priority.

| Priority | Description | Examples | Target first action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | Serious legal, safety, fee or data protection issue | Illegal recruitment fee, fake job, document misuse, sanctions concern, serious privacy incident | Immediate review, ideally within 24 hours |
| High | Material harm or significant contractual issue | Misleading job terms, partner misconduct, payment dispute, discrimination allegation | Review within 2 business days |
| Medium | Operational issue requiring correction | Incorrect profile, document status dispute, AI matching error | Review within 5 business days |
| Low | General feedback or minor issue | Website issue, wording correction, non-urgent support | Review within 10 business days |

## 9. Acknowledgement

CrewPortGlobal should acknowledge receipt of a complaint as soon as reasonably possible.

Recommended acknowledgement period:

- Critical complaints: within 24 hours.
- Other complaints: within 3 business days.

The acknowledgement should include:

- case number;
- date received;
- complaint category;
- expected review timeline;
- request for missing information, if required;
- statement that retaliation against complainants is not permitted.

## 10. Investigation process

The reviewer should follow these steps:

1. Confirm the identity and role of the complainant where necessary.
2. Check whether the complaint relates to CrewPortGlobal, a shipowner, a partner or an external actor.
3. Review user profile, GTC_USER_ID, documents, chat records, request records and payment records where relevant.
4. Request additional information from the complainant if required.
5. Contact the relevant shipowner, partner or internal team member where appropriate.
6. Check whether the issue involves prohibited recruitment fees, privacy risk, sanctions risk or safety risk.
7. Suspend or pause affected workflows where necessary.
8. Prepare findings.
9. Decide corrective action.
10. Record the outcome in the complaint register.

## 11. Special rule for recruitment fee complaints

Any complaint alleging that a seafarer was asked to pay a recruitment, placement or employment fee must be treated as high priority or critical priority.

CrewPortGlobal must review:

- who requested the payment;
- whether the payment was connected to job access, placement or employment;
- whether the payment was made to CrewPortGlobal, a partner, a shipowner, an intermediary or an unknown party;
- whether the service was optional or presented as mandatory;
- whether any public or contractual statement was misleading;
- whether other seafarers may be affected.

If a prohibited fee was requested by a partner or representative, CrewPortGlobal may:

- suspend the partner;
- remove the related vacancy or request;
- notify affected users;
- refund any prohibited charge collected by CrewPortGlobal by error;
- terminate the partner relationship;
- escalate to legal counsel or competent authority where required.

CrewPortGlobal must not retaliate against any seafarer for reporting a suspected prohibited fee.

## 12. Interim protective measures

Depending on the risk, CrewPortGlobal may take interim action before final resolution.

Possible measures:

- pause a candidate submission;
- hide or suspend a vacancy;
- suspend a partner account;
- restrict document access;
- freeze a workflow;
- require human review before further processing;
- place a warning flag on a company or profile;
- notify affected users of a security or fraud concern;
- preserve evidence.

Interim measures do not mean that the complaint is proven. They are used to protect users and preserve the integrity of the process.

## 13. Outcomes and corrective actions

Possible outcomes include:

- complaint upheld;
- complaint partially upheld;
- complaint not upheld;
- insufficient information;
- referred to partner;
- referred to legal review;
- referred to competent authority;
- technical correction completed;
- user data corrected or deleted;
- document status updated;
- payment refunded;
- partner suspended or terminated;
- shipowner account restricted;
- internal process improved.

The outcome must be recorded in the complaint register.

## 14. Response to complainant

The final response should include:

- case number;
- summary of the issue reviewed;
- decision or outcome;
- corrective action where appropriate;
- any limitations on what can be disclosed due to confidentiality;
- escalation or appeal option, if available.

CrewPortGlobal should not disclose confidential information about other users, companies or partners unless legally permitted and necessary.

## 15. Escalation

A complaint must be escalated to senior management, legal counsel or compliance review if it involves:

- alleged illegal recruitment fee;
- suspected human trafficking, forced labour or exploitation;
- serious maritime safety concern;
- sanctions or prohibited party risk;
- misuse of identity documents;
- data breach or sensitive data exposure;
- criminal fraud;
- threat, coercion or retaliation;
- regulator or authority contact;
- media or reputational risk.

## 16. Appeals and review

If the complainant disagrees with the outcome, they may request review within 14 calendar days of receiving the final response.

An appeal should be reviewed by a person who was not the original reviewer where possible.

The appeal decision should be recorded in the complaint register.

## 17. Record retention

Complaint records should be retained for a defined period in accordance with CrewPortGlobal Data Retention and Deletion Policy.

Recommended minimum retention:

- general complaints: 3 years after closure;
- recruitment fee, fraud, sanctions, serious misconduct or legal complaints: 6 years after closure or longer if required by law;
- data protection complaints: according to applicable data protection rules and legal advice.

Records may be retained longer if there is a legal hold, dispute, investigation or regulator request.

## 18. Data protection

Complaint handling may involve sensitive personal data and professional documents.

CrewPortGlobal must:

- limit access to complaint records;
- process only necessary data;
- protect uploaded evidence;
- avoid unnecessary disclosure;
- respect user privacy rights;
- record any data correction or deletion request;
- treat identity documents and certificates as confidential.

## 19. AI use in complaint handling

AI tools may be used to:

- summarize complaint information;
- classify complaint category;
- identify missing information;
- prepare draft responses;
- identify relevant policy sections.

AI must not be the final decision-maker for serious complaints.

Human review is required for:

- recruitment fee complaints;
- fraud complaints;
- discrimination complaints;
- sanctions risks;
- document misuse;
- data protection complaints;
- account suspension;
- partner termination;
- any complaint with legal or safety implications.

## 20. Anti-retaliation rule

CrewPortGlobal prohibits retaliation against any person who submits a complaint in good faith.

Retaliation may include:

- blocking access to job opportunities because of a complaint;
- lowering profile visibility because of a complaint;
- threatening a seafarer;
- refusing service without legitimate reason;
- sharing complaint details to harm the complainant.

Any suspected retaliation should be treated as a separate complaint.

If you believe that you were asked to pay a recruitment or placement fee, received misleading job information, experienced misuse of your documents, or have any concern about the platform, a shipowner or a partner, please contact us. CrewPortGlobal does not charge seafarers recruitment, placement or employment fees.
