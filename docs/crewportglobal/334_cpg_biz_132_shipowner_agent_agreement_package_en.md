## SHIPOWNER-AGENT AGREEMENT No. {{contract_number}}

Place: {{contract_city}}  
Date: {{contract_date}}

{{shipowner_legal_name}}, represented by {{shipowner_signer_name}}, acting on the basis of {{shipowner_signer_basis}}, hereinafter referred to as the "Principal", and {{agent_legal_name}}, represented by {{agent_signer_name}}, acting on the basis of {{agent_signer_basis}}, hereinafter referred to as the "Agent", together the "Parties", agree as follows.

## 1. Subject Matter

1.1. The Agent shall, on the Principal's instruction and in the Principal's interests, provide crew-search, candidate presentation, document coordination, interview, joining, request-support and related services for the Principal's vessels, crew requests and candidate workflows.

1.2. The Agent may act only within this Agreement, Appendix 2 (Power of Attorney / Authority Document), the delegated scope recorded in CrewPortGlobal and any signed Service Order or commercial appendix.

1.3. The seafarer employment agreement ("SEA") is a separate agreement between the seafarer and the shipowner, employer or other duly authorized employer-side party. This Agreement does not replace the SEA, applicable law, collective bargaining agreement or mandatory maritime labour protections.

1.4. This Agreement is a CrewPortGlobal standard framework adhesion-form agreement package. It regulates legal relationship, authority, restrictions, no-fee boundary, platform governance, notifications, data responsibility and the one-active-manager rule.

1.5. Acceptance of this Agreement does not by itself agree a fixed service price, success fee, SLA penalty or billing basis. Commercial terms are agreed separately through a Service Order, commercial appendix, request, order form or approved price-basis record.

## 2. Definitions

2.1. "CrewPortGlobal" means the platform and workflow system operated for structured maritime crew data, matching, documents, contract preparation and participant governance.

2.2. "Request" means the Principal's instruction for crew search, candidate presentation or related work.

2.3. "Candidate" means a seafarer considered, shortlisted or presented for a Request.

2.4. "Successful joining" means verified arrival and commencement of service by the Candidate on the relevant vessel or employment context.

2.5. "Delegated scope" means the object or workflow area that the Principal authorizes the Agent to manage in CrewPortGlobal.

2.6. "Authority Document" means Appendix 2, a power of attorney or other platform-approved authority evidence.

2.7. "Commercial Terms Pending" means the Parties have accepted the legal framework but have not yet agreed the paid service basis, price, success fee, SLA economics or invoice basis.

## 3. Agent Duties And Authority Boundaries

3.1. The Agent may perform the following duties where included in the delegated scope:

- □ receive and review Requests;
- □ identify and present candidate shortlists;
- □ coordinate candidate documents;
- □ organize interviews and term clarification;
- □ coordinate joining logistics;
- □ prepare stage reports and closing documents;
- □ prepare contract workspace records without final SEA signature authority unless separately granted.

3.2. The Agent may not, without separate express written authority:

- □ sign a seafarer employment agreement on behalf of the Principal;
- □ change a seafarer's wage, allotment, deduction or repatriation terms;
- □ issue financial guarantees or payment obligations on behalf of the Principal;
- □ transfer, receive or use the Principal's password, email code or personal account credentials;
- □ create a second active manager for the same delegated object or scope.

3.3. The Agent must act in good faith and in accordance with recorded authority, applicable law, platform controls and the legitimate interests of represented parties.

## 4. Offer, Acceptance And Platform Appointment

4.1. Inside CrewPortGlobal, the Principal first sends an offer to the Agent to conclude this Agreement and receive authority for a defined object or scope.

4.2. Sending an offer does not create active appointment and does not transfer operational management.

4.3. The Agent may accept the offer only inside CrewPortGlobal by signing this Agreement package or by approved electronic acceptance confirming:

- □ I agree to the Agent Agreement, Authority Document / power of attorney, mandatory appendices and CrewPortGlobal portal appointment rules;
- □ I understand that acceptance covers the full contract package and not merely a short reference page or link;
- □ the Agent signatory authority is confirmed;
- □ commercial terms are agreed separately unless already fixed in a Service Order, commercial appendix, request or approved price-basis record;
- □ the Authority Document must be issued and recorded in CrewPortGlobal where authority is required.

4.4. CrewPortGlobal may activate Agent management only after:

1. the Principal has sent the in-platform offer;
2. the Agent has accepted this Agreement package;
3. the Authority Document has been issued or recorded where applicable;
4. mandatory appendices and checklist are accepted;
5. signatory authority is confirmed;
6. the one-active-manager check passes;
7. the participant notification ledger records are created.

4.5. External agreements, emails or powers of attorney may be uploaded as evidence for Platform Administration / Control review. They do not automatically activate platform management unless the CrewPortGlobal assignment process is completed.
